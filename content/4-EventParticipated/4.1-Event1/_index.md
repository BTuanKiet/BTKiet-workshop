### Key highlights

#### The importance of context in AI

- AI often fails not because the model is weak, but because the user does not provide enough context.
- Context turns a vague request into a concrete task by adding goals, situations, and constraints.
- Effective AI usage should move beyond simple **prompting** toward stronger **context** and **memory**.

#### AI assistants with Amazon Q

- The event introduced **Amazon Q** capabilities for data analysis, reporting, and natural-language workflows.
- Features such as chat, flows, spaces, and insights help teams share knowledge and complete tasks faster.

#### Building infrastructure with Amazon CloudFront

- CloudFront helps optimize data transfer costs, improve performance, and reduce latency for end users.
- **AWS Shield**, **AWS WAF**, and **Origin Cloaking** strengthen security, mitigate DDoS risks, and hide origin infrastructure from the public internet.
- Multi-layer caching and HTTP/3 support improve the end-user experience.

#### Multi-Agent systems and enterprise AI infrastructure

- The **Multi-Agent** model breaks complex work into specialized agents that can handle focused tasks more effectively.
- A multi-agent architecture supports result cross-checking, control, and transparent audit trails.
- Managing data, context, and token cost is essential when operating AI systems in enterprise environments.

### Key takeaways

- **Context Quality > Context Quantity:** Providing the right evidence matters more than adding too much noisy information.
- **Enterprise-grade mindset:** Security, compliance, and operations should be considered from the beginning of an AI system.
- Learned how to reduce repetitive LLM behavior by tuning parameters and designing better prompt/context structures.
- Learned how **CloudFront Functions** can process logic at the edge and reduce load on origin servers.
- Gained teamwork lessons from Team VIB's LotusHacks experience under time pressure.

### Applying to work

- Apply a four-part context framework: **Goal, Info, Constraints, Success Criteria** when working with AI.
- Deploy **CloudFront** for current projects to improve security and optimize bandwidth cost.
- Experiment with **Multi-Agent** structures for complex analytical tasks instead of relying on a single agent.
- Use **Amazon Q** to simplify report generation from specialized datasets.

### Event experience

- Attending FCAJ Community Day at Bitexco Financial Tower was an inspiring learning experience.
- I listened directly to experts explaining how they solved real enterprise problems at organizations such as VPBank and Cloud Kinetics.
- Technical demos such as UTMorpho and Bedrock Playground configuration helped me better understand how AI products are built.
- The event also helped me understand career paths and how students can start building AI products.

### Lessons learned

- **AI does not replace humans**, but people who know how to use AI and manage context well will have a strong advantage.
- **No system is perfect:** AI systems should be designed to handle probabilistic results and errors.
- **Infrastructure is the foundation:** A strong AI application needs reliable infrastructure such as CloudFront to ensure performance and availability.
